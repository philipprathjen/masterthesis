Section I: Aghion et al. (2005)

The following graph displays the relationship between the innvoation level within the economy and the collusion/competition parameter developed by Aghion et al. (2005)

    The escape-competition effect: If two companies compete directly on the same level, fiercer competition will increase their incentive to get ahead and capture rents
    The Schumpeterian effect: Reducing the rents that can be captured by a follower if he innovates, reduces his incentive to innovate

Recall that an incumbent cannot innovate by assumption in the Aghion et al. (2005) model.

The graphic makes clear how an inverted-U relationship depending on the relationship $ \eta=\frac{h}{\sqrt{\pi_{1}}} $

Specifically, the paramterized model above allows easy tractability of the fact the inverted U shape will be generated whenever:

$$ \sqrt{\eta^{2}+1}<\sqrt{(\eta^{2}+2)/3}+\eta<\sqrt{\eta^{2}+2} $$

Furthermore, as the Appendix in the Aghion. et. al (2005) makes clear, the escape competition effect will sctrictly dominate whenever:

$$ \sqrt{(\eta^{2}+2)/3}+\eta\geq\sqrt{\eta^{2}+2} $$

The Schumpeterian effect will strictly dominate within the economy whenever:

$$ \sqrt{\eta^{2}+1}>\sqrt{(\eta^{2}+2)/3}+\eta $$
